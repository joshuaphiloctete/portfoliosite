Joshua Philoctete

Designer and Developer.

Let's create something amazing.

My name is Joshua Philoctete and I am a third-year Digital Arts and Sciences Student studying at the University of Florida. I am driven and self-motivated, and am constantly looking to expand my knowledge in new technologies and techniques. I am passionate about user experience design and front-end web development. I love crafting beautiful websites and designs that effortlessly convey a message to a user or viewer.
I also have a strong interest in augmented and virtual reality development, and spend a lot of time experimenting with new ideas using Unity3D, and well as with mixed reality web technologies such as A-Frame and AR.js.
I am currently working as a Marketing Intern with the City of Gainesville, where I work on the conception and implementation of marketing strategies, as well as on various design and advertising projects.
Please feel free to contact me if you have any questions!

AR/VR Projects

This is an augmented reality Valentine's Day card experience built using Unity3D and the Vuforia SDK. It uses image tracking to follow the message inside the card and display 3D content on top of it, as well as to play sound and to display particle effects. The code for the app was written in C#.

This is a screen recording of my current progress on a virtual reality space experience that I am working on. It is intended to become a game in which you fire lazers at the incoming asteroids. It is built for Google Cardboard (Android) using Unity, with the code being written in C#. I acquired the assets through the Unity Asset Store, and am solely responsible for the level design and coding. The finished version of the app will be uploaded very soon!

3D Animation

This is my final project for my 3D animation class! It was made using Autodesk Maya 2018. I used the Norman rig and a few other assets to tell a short story about a guy who just can't quite seem to pick up a basket! The animation, modeling of laundry basket, and textures were done by me. The house furniture models were courtesy of Turbosquid, and the music was downloaded from Freesound.org.
